Title description, Dec 7, 2017

Small Gorgan is a mountain in the Ukrainian Carpathians, in the array of Gorgany. It is located in the Nadvirna district of Ivano-Frankivsk region, north of the village of Polyanitsy and west of the village of Tatarov. The height of 1592.5 m. Northeastern slopes are very steep, difficult passes. The summit and the upland slopes are unclosed, with stone deposits, in places - curvilinear from the mountain pine; below - forest arrays. Mountain Maly Gorgan, along with the neighboring summit - Synyak (1665 m) - is part of the Cynac ridge and is located in its northwestern part. The nearest settlements: Polyanytsya, Tatariv.

If you move from Sinai, then on the top of Maly Gorgan there is a fork of two routes. One goes straight down the ridge down to the Zelenitsa river (on the maps marked with the name of Ilma). And then to the village of Green, either in the direction of Dovbushanka. Another route returns in relation to the ridge to the left, under 90 degrees, towards the Blajiv valley and the Babyn Pohar Mountains, and then to the Table Pass or to the valley near Dovbushanka. The first descent takes place mainly on a large stone and only in the forest zone the stitch has a serious bow, which sometimes has to cling to the roots of the trees. The second descent (southern) in the length of about 150-250 meters is a shallow stone spill, which is a certain complexity, if during the descent it is raining.

Synyak is a high, steep sloping mountain in Gorgany (Ukrainian Carpathians). It is located within the Carpathian National Nature Park, in the Nadvirna district of Ivano-Frankivsk region, not far from the villages of Polyanytsya and Tatariv. Height - 1665 m. To a height of about 1300 m, the mountain is covered with forest, above alpine meadows, and even after 100 m - solid rock mass. The array of mountains extends from the southeast to the northwest. Mount Sinyak, along with a neighboring peak called the Small Gorgan (1592 m), is part of the Syniak ridge. Mount Syniak is a popular hiking trail on the weekend. From its summit, the adjoining ridges are visible: Dovbushanka (in the northwest) and Yavirnyk (in the northeast). This also offers a magnificent panorama of the ski resort Bukovel, as well as a view of the highest ridge of the Ukrainian Carpathians - Chornohirsky, with the peaks of Hoverla (2061 m) and Petros (2020 m). Mount Syniak, perhaps, is nicknamed so because of its color - through the thickness of the mountain air, covered with gray moss, the stones appear blue.

Zaroslyak-city of Pozhgewska-Lake Nesamovit-Spitz-Hajin

Title description, Sep 2, 2017

Start from Zaroslyak through the city of Pozzhevsk to Spits, after 7 hours in Kolyba on the valley in the lower Hajin. The next morning, start before 10:00 on Spitsa through Hajin, and return to Zaroslyak despite the Unthankful, finish on Zaroslyak.

Lake Nesamovit, 88 meters long, 45 meters wide, 1.5 meters deep, is one of the highest lakes in the list. It is worth noting that the Nesamovite lake is of glacial origin, when the air temperature drops below zero, the water of the lake freezes. They say that even in the summer on hot days the water does not warm up. The area of ​​this "mountain mirror" is 0.3 hectares.
